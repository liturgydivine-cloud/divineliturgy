The Liturgy of the Faithful

"Anaphora" is a Greek word meaning "offering," that is, the offering of the oblations (Al-Qarabeen), meaning the Divine Liturgy [1]. Also, the word "Liturgy" (Liturgia) means service, or collective prayers and rituals, but the consensus has settled on it being the specific term for the Divine Liturgy.

Summary:

I spoke about the Liturgy being two parts: the Liturgy of the Word (Al-Quddas Al-Ta'limi) or the Liturgy of the Catechumens (Quddas Al-Maw'uzin), and the second part is the Liturgy of the Faithful (Quddas Al-Mu'minin) or the Anaphora, which I will begin explaining today by the grace of Christ.

The Liturgy of the Word consisted of three parts, which are:

  1. The Prayer of the Psalms (Agpeya).
  2. The Choosing (Ikhtiyar) and Signs (Rushumat) of the Lamb.
  3. The Readings (Al-Qira'at): which are the Pauline Epistle (Al-Bolis), the Catholic Epistle (Al-Catholicon), the Acts of the Apostles (Al-Praxis), the Synaxarium (Al-Synaxar), followed by the Gospel and the Sermon (Al-Wa'za).

The Liturgy of the Faithful (Quddas Al-Mu'minin) / The Anaphora

Starting today, by the grace of God, we will begin explaining the Liturgy of the Faithful or the Anaphora. We divide it into seven stations or stages until we reach the meeting with Christ and the partaking of His Precious Body and Pure Blood.

The Seven Stations:

  1. First Station: The Prayer of Reconciliation (Salat Al-Solh) or the Pre-Anaphoral Prayer.
  2. Second Station: The Prayer "It is Meet and Right" (Mostahaq wa Adel). In it, the priest speaks about the being of God before all ages, the Creator of all things, sitting upon the throne of His glory. Then he begins to speak about those around the throne standing before Him from the angelic powers. We will explain this station in detail because it is important, as we have begun to approach the throne of God.
  3. Third Station: "Agios" (Quddus, Quddus, Quddus - Holy, Holy, Holy). In it, Abouna speaks to God, and it ends with the Sanctification (Al-Taqdis) and the descent of the Holy Spirit, transforming the bread and wine into the Body and Blood of the Lord Jesus Christ, making Him present in the church and on the Altar.
  4. Fourth Station: The Litanies (Al-Awashi) or Supplications.
  5. Fifth Station: The Commemoration (Al-Magma') or the Assembly of Saints, headed by our Mother the Virgin.
  6. Sixth Station: The Fraction (Al-Qisma), where Abouna begins breaking the Body, which symbolizes the sufferings of the Lord Jesus Christ. (Notice that during the Fraction throughout the year, "Kyrie Eleison" is chanted in a mournful tune / Lahn Hazayni because these are the moments of the sufferings and wounds of the Lord Jesus Christ).
  7. Seventh Station: The moment of Communion (Al-Tanawul) from the Body and Blood of the Lord Jesus Christ. It is the stage of meeting and encountering the Lord Jesus Christ and the preparation when the deacon says: "Pray for worthy partaking of these Holy Mysteries."

The First Station: The Prayer of Reconciliation (Salat Al-Solh)

The Pre-Anaphoral Prayer, or according to the Coptic Rite, the Prayer of the Holy Kiss (Al-Qubla Al-Muqaddasa / Reconciliation).

Before the Prayer of Reconciliation, the entire church prays the Creed (Qanun Al-Iman). During this time, Abouna begins to wash his hands in preparation for the service. At the same time, Abouna takes a little water in his hand and sprinkles it before the people as a warning and alert to anyone partaking unworthily and not living a life of repentance [2].

The Prayer of Reconciliation begins while the Altar is covered by the Prospharin (Al-Prospharin), representing the stone. Of course, under the Prospharin, the Paten (Siniya) is covered with a veil containing the Lamb, and the Chalice (Kass) containing the wine is covered with a veil. All of this symbolizes the tomb and its seals.

Why do we call it the Prayer of Reconciliation? Because sin separated us from God, and there was no peace between Heaven and earth, and we were far from God. Therefore, there must be reconciliation because of what the Lord of Glory, Jesus Christ, did and the salvation He accomplished on the Cross. We must be prepared before the lifting of the stone (the Prospharin) and the seals (the triangular veil that Abouna holds in the second part of the Prayer of Reconciliation).

  1. The First Part of the Prayer of Reconciliation: It is considered a vertical part, from earth to Heaven, where we reconcile with God.
  2. The Second Part of the Prayer of Reconciliation: It is horizontal, where everyone present in the church reconciles with each other. It concerns the people present in the church because the Lord Jesus Christ said: "Therefore if you bring your gift to the altar, and there remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there before the altar, and go your way. First be reconciled to your brother, and then come and offer your gift." (Matthew 5:23-24). This is the horizontal reconciliation, the reconciliation among the worshipers.

The teachings of the Apostles say: It is the responsibility of those entering the fellowship of the Eucharist (Eucharistia) to be guardians over one another. They should not allow anyone they know to be living in sin or in a dispute with another to come forward to partake with them, because an impure fellowship defiles the rest as well.

That is why we must begin with the Prayer of Reconciliation. Because it is the gate, or the "security checkpoint," that we must ensure we pass through. We cannot enter and complete the rest of the stations of the Liturgy, stand before the Body and Blood of the Lord Jesus Christ, and enter into Communion while there is a dispute, upset, or misunderstanding between us. Please think about this moment. We must enter the church without our hearts holding or carrying any upset, grudge, or hatred toward anyone.

This is a very important part because Abouna, in the Prayer of Reconciliation, declares our reconciliation with God first through the Lord Jesus Christ, and then our reconciliation with each other in the second part of the Prayer of Reconciliation through the Holy Kiss (Al-Qubla Al-Muqaddasa).

The First Part (The Vertical Part from Earth to Heaven)

Abouna begins the Prayer of Reconciliation with his hands bare, not covered by any veil (Lafa'if). This signifies the bare state of humanity before God until the completion of reconciliation on the Cross. Because of the sin that stripped humanity in the person of Adam and Eve, he prays with bare hands because the priest here represents humanity that suffered from the nakedness of sin. We all know that Adam and Eve, after sinning, knew they were naked.

The Prayer begins when Abouna says:

"O Great and Eternal God, who formed man in incorruption, and death which entered into the world by the envy of the devil..."

The first Prayer of Reconciliation is entirely contemplations on God's creation of man in His image and likeness without corruption. But man fell through the envy of the devil and could not keep God's commandment. The result, we all know, is that the sentence of death was passed upon us. But this sentence ended with the life-giving manifestation of His Only-Begotten Son, the Lord Jesus Christ, who accomplished salvation on the Cross. And when the Lord Jesus Christ was incarnated, He filled the earth with heavenly peace, as the angels chanted, saying: "Glory to God in the highest, and on earth peace, goodwill toward men!" Remember this praise that the angels said to the shepherds?

At the end of the First Part, the Deacon alerts the people to pray, and for each one to examine his heart and see if he is ready for the next step. Because up until now, we are still standing at the "security checkpoint."

  • The Deacon says: "Pray for perfect peace, love, and the holy apostolic kisses."
  • The people respond and say: "Lord have mercy."

Meaning, you are asking for God's mercies for the forgiveness of sins. (This is a fulfillment of the commandment from the Gospel: "Since you have purified your souls in obeying the truth through the Spirit in sincere love of the brethren, love one another fervently with a pure heart." - 1 Peter 1:22).

The Second Part (The Horizontal Prayer)

Abouna begins the Second Part and holds the triangular veil (or removes the seal). He holds it with both hands and places it before his eyes [3]. On the other side of the Altar, you find a deacon holding a Cross.

The meaning of this is that our sin was present and prevented our eyes from seeing the salvation of Christ, because sin blinds the eyes and hides God from our sight. But the salvation of Christ is present—the salvation He accomplished on the Cross, which the deacon on the opposite side of Abouna is holding—and the work of the Cross in salvation. As soon as Abouna removes the veil from his eyes at the end of the prayer, he will see the Cross and the salvation of the Cross.

Abouna begins the Second Part, which is the Horizontal Prayer, meaning reconciliation with each other. He says:

"O God, in Your good pleasure, fill our hearts with Your peace."

(Take note: peace is priceless, whether it is inner peace or spiritual peace; these are what is important in the life of a Christian). He wants there to be peace among everyone present in the church or outside the church.

Abouna continues his request and says:

"And cleanse us from all blemish, all guile, all hypocrisy, all malice, and the remembrance of evil entailing death."

(Thinking about past sins with which the devil fights us is the "remembrance of evil" that Abouna asks to be removed from our minds).

He asks God to cleanse us from all the aforementioned traits. He asks for purity, and we remember the saying of Saint Anba Roweis: "Who is worthy of Communion unless their inner being is pure like the inner being of the Virgin Mary?"

Then Abouna prays to God that we may be reconciled with each other and offer proof of this by greeting one another with a holy kiss. This is so that we may reach the end of the Liturgy, stand before the Body and Blood of Christ without condemnation, and be able to receive this Divine Gift (the Body and Blood of the Lord Jesus Christ) worthily.

When Abouna says "in Christ Jesus our Lord," the lifting of the Prospharin (Al-Prospharin) begins.

The Lifting of the Prospharin & The Holy Kiss

From the other side, the partner priest or the deacon holding the Cross helps Abouna lift the Prospharin. This signifies the opening of the tomb and the Resurrection of the Lord of Glory. The Prospharin should ideally have small bells to make a sound, signifying the rolling away of the stone from the door of the tomb and the earthquake that occurred at the time of the Glorious Resurrection. Meaning, it is a declaration of the Resurrection of the Lord of Glory from the dead.

Here, reconciliation is completed and realized, and we now have a place among the heavenly beings, sharing with them in praise and thanksgiving in the presence of the Lord's Resurrection.

Pay attention to this moment: It is one of the most terrifying times for the devil, when he is reminded of his defeat before the Lord of Glory. When He rose, He raised us with Him and broke the sting of death.

After lifting the Prospharin, the deacon holding the Cross says, to complete the Prayer of Reconciliation:

"Greet one another with a holy kiss. Lord have mercy, Lord have mercy, Lord have mercy." (He repeats it three times for emphasis and to draw down God's mercy). "Yea, Lord, who is Jesus Christ the Son of God, hear us and have mercy upon us. Offer in order. Stand with trembling. Look towards the East. Let us attend."

The deacon says this because at this moment there used to be commotion and noise in the church; the deacon warns and asks for quietness.

The Lord Jesus Christ made reconciliation and peace on earth, and reconciled the heavenly with the earthly. This kiss is a sign of the union of the members of the Church into one body and one spirit. According to the Didascalia [4], men do not kiss women; rather, a man greets his brother, and women greet women. In the book The Precious Jewel in the Rite of the Church regarding the manner of the kiss in the Liturgy: one would kiss the other on the right side of the neck, then shake hands, and the other would return the same. This was in the early Church.

Saint Justin Martyr (a saint from the 2nd century AD) said that the liturgical kiss confirms that those present desire and want to truly be one family. The Holy Kiss is a sign of love and fellowship within the One Body of the Lord Jesus Christ, which is the Church. The Eucharist is the love of Christ poured into us; if not for our love for one another, we would not dare to approach it. It is a Mystery of Thanksgiving from within the Mystery of Love. For the Church to be a path or a bridge over which the love of Christ crosses to the world, love must be perfect and strong in the Church (the congregation of believers).

After the Holy Kiss, the people cry out asking for the intercession of the Virgin Mary, saying:

"Through the intercessions of the Theotokos, Saint Mary, O Lord, grant us the forgiveness of our sins..."

Because the Lord Jesus Christ is connected to the Virgin Mary; through her He was born and incarnated, and through her the divine reconciliation was accomplished. Furthermore, her intercession is acceptable before her Son and her Lord. (Remember the Wedding in Cana of Galilee: because of her intercession, the Lord Jesus Christ turned the water into wine).

(Note: In the Rite of the Church, the ordination of priests and deacons takes place before the Holy Kiss).

The Last Thing (Which is Very Important)

Pay attention to this, as you might not notice it because Abouna's back is to you. Abouna takes the triangular Veil (Lafa'if)—or the seal—that was on the Prospharin with his left hand and keeps it on his left hand. He takes the veil that was on the Paten (Siniya) with his right hand and keeps it on his right hand. The Chalice remains covered.

This signifies that His Divinity never parted from His Humanity, even while He was in the tomb, but remained united with the Body [5].

With this, we have finished the First Station in the Divine Liturgy, which is the Prayer of Reconciliation. And to our God be all glory and honor forever. Amen.
