The Ritual & Spiritual Aspects of the Fraction

Summary of the Previous Lesson:

As I said last time, the Prayer of the Fraction (Salat Al-Qisma) will take more than a week to explain. The Prayer of the Fraction is the distribution of the portion and inheritance of the Lord Jesus Christ, because He gives a portion and an inheritance to all who partake of His Body and Blood. And as I also said last time, His Body is not divided or fragmented, because any small particle (Gawhara) you take from the Body is the complete Body.

I said that the Fraction begins from the Prayers of Institution (Al-Salawat Al-Ta'sisiyya) when Abouna takes the Body with his right hand and places it on his left hand. I explained this meaning to you: the right hand symbolizes the Jews, and the left hand symbolizes the Gentiles, because the Jews handed the Lord Jesus Christ over to the Gentiles to be judged. "For He will be delivered to the Gentiles and will be mocked and insulted and spit upon. They will scourge Him and kill Him. And the third day He will rise again." (Luke 18:32-33).

Abouna begins dividing the Lamb from top to bottom, symbolizing the descent of the Lord Jesus Christ from His glory for the salvation of the world. He divides it without completely separating it, dividing a part above the Spadikon (Al-Isbadikon) and a part below the Spadikon, because the Lord Jesus Christ has no beginning and no end; He is the First and the Last, as stated in the Book of Revelation. And as stated in the Book of Genesis, the upper part is called the Head, and the lower part is called the Feet, as "the tribe of Judah is the head and the tail."

  • The part on the right (one-third of the Lamb) symbolizes the Son, and the part on the left (two-thirds) symbolizes the Father and the Holy Spirit.
  • Or, another interpretation: the one-third represents the institution of the Sacrament in the Upper Room, and the two-thirds represent the completion of the Sacrament on the Cross and the Resurrection.

This is the first stage of the Fraction. After Abouna finishes, the deacon calls out, saying: "Worship God in fear and trembling." The whole church prostrates (Yasjud), and the Holy Spirit descends upon the Oblations (Al-Qarabeen), transforming them into the true Body of the Lord Jesus Christ, and upon the wine in the Chalice (Kass), transforming it into the true Blood of Emmanuel our God.

Before the prayer of completing the Fraction, Abouna prays and says: "Let us give thanks to God the Pantocrator." I explained why we thank our Lord: that He hosted us in His house and made us worthy to stand in this holy place, and because He is present with His Body and Blood on the Altar. We call the Church the "House of God." It also reminds us to pray and be broken by lifting our hands because we are truly in the presence of God. I explained this part in detail.

In this prayer, the whole church prostrates to the Holy Body and the Precious Blood. The Body is signed with the Blood from the Chalice using the index finger in the shape of a cross, as I explained last time. At the end of the prayer, Abouna says: "Peace be with all of you" without making the sign of the cross, because this peace comes directly from the Lord Jesus Christ.

I spoke with you last time about important information regarding the Fraction:

  1. Signing the Body with the Blood: Indicates the union of His Divinity with His Humanity, and that this Blood is for this Body, and this Body is for this Blood.
  2. When Abouna says "The Precious Blood": This represents the crucifixion of Christ, the shedding of His Blood, and the offering of Himself for the world. Therefore, the people cry out and say: "Lord have mercy."
  3. The Fraction is the peak of love: Because the Lord Jesus Christ shows His love for the Church, who is the Bride, and reminds her how He endured torments and sufferings and shed His blood on the Cross for the sake of the Church. During the Fraction, we must remember this love.
  4. The division does not fall upon the actual Body of our Lord Jesus: But upon the bread—the material used that represents the Body to complete this Sacrament. Just as water is used in Baptism and oil in the prayer of the Holy Unction.
  5. The particle (Gawhara) that you receive in Communion is the complete Body of the Lord Jesus Christ: Not a part of it. The division falls upon the accidents (Al-'Arad), which is the bread, because "not one of His bones was broken."
  6. The sufferings of the Lord Jesus Christ did not touch His Divinity: Because the Divinity is incapable of physical suffering. As for the Pure Body itself, it is not divided because He is All in All, and He is present in His entirety, whole, in every part of the bread and wine.
  7. The Fraction was compared to a word spoken by a person: Another person hears it, and if many people hear it, this word does not diminish; it reaches every one of them complete. Thus, the breaking of the bread, whether large or small, is the Body of Christ.
  8. Dividing the Body into 12 parts: This is the number of the disciples of the Lord of Glory, and with them the Savior—a large cross in the center called the Spadikon (Al-Isbadikon).
  9. The Fraction has a spiritual meaning: It is the separation of the soul of the Lord Jesus Christ from His body, as happened on the Cross when He yielded up His spirit.
  10. Dividing the Body signifies the sufferings that befell the Lord Jesus Christ: Such as the scourging, carrying the Cross, being pierced with the spear, the crown of thorns, driving the nails, and being struck on the face.
  11. The separations that Abouna makes between the particles are called "wounds": Look at how many wounds were on the body of Christ! "But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities; the chastisement for our peace was upon Him, and by His stripes we are healed." (Isaiah 53:5).
  12. The Prayer of the Fraction consists of thanksgivings to God for this inexpressible gift: He gave us His Holy Body and Precious Blood so that we may live by them forever, if we partake of them with repentance and a broken, humble, and contrite heart.

The Ritual & Spiritual Aspects of the Fraction (Al-Qisma)

Today we will take the Fraction from the ritual (Taqsi) perspective and the spiritual perspective.

The Prayers of the Fraction are many. Some are annual (Sanawi), and some are specific to the Lord's Feasts (Al-A'yad Al-Sayyidiyya), the feasts of the Virgin, the Angels, the Saints, and the Fasts. The number of Fraction prayers in the Church is 20, plus the specific Fraction prayers for the Basil, Cyril, and Gregory Liturgies, making a total of 23 Fractions [1].

The Ritual Stages of Dividing the Holy Body:

  1. The First Stage: This is the Prayer of Institution (Salat Al-Ta'sees), which begins when Abouna says, "And He broke it."
  2. The Second Stage: This takes place after the descent of the Holy Spirit. It is a holy rite interspersed with the responses of the people asking for mercy when they say three times: "Lord have mercy" (Kyrie Eleison). The tune is mournful (Hazayni) throughout the year, to align with the sufferings and wounds of the Lord Jesus Christ.

There are two rites (Taquus) for the Fraction in the Coptic Church: The first is the Connected Fraction, and the second is the Disconnected (Separated) Fraction.

First: The Connected Fraction (Al-Qisma Al-Muttasila): In this rite, the priest divides the Holy Body without separating the parts from one another completely. This Fraction is not widespread, but some priests pray it according to the tradition (Tasleem) they received during their 40-day retreat [2].

Second: The Separated Fraction (Al-Qisma Al-Munfasila): Abouna begins the Prayer of the Fraction. He divides the Pure Body into one-third and two-thirds, and places the one-third on top of the two-thirds in the shape of the Holy Cross.

Then Abouna takes the particle (Gawhara) that is above the two-thirds and places it in the Paten (Siniya) on the eastern side. He takes the particle that is below the two-thirds and places it in the western side. Meaning, he separates the "Head" and the "Feet" from the two-thirds.

Then Abouna separates a particle with a cross from the top of the one-third and places it on the right. The remainder of the one-third he places on the left side of the Paten. Abouna takes the two-thirds, and the particles in the Paten now form the shape of the Cross.

Then Abouna separates the part containing the Spadikon (Al-Isbadikon) from top to bottom. He divides the two-thirds in his hand into four particles. After that, he takes the part he placed on the left into his hand, and puts the part divided into four particles in its place. Then he begins dividing the three particles in the one-third under each cross.

Then Abouna holds the part containing the Spadikon and carefully tries to separate it intact, along with a part of the inner crumb (Al-Lubaba). He kisses it and puts it back in its place.

After dividing the Body, Abouna gathers and reassembles all the parts of the Pure Body that he divided, so its shape returns to how it was at the beginning. Then he rubs his hands inside the Paten. Then he lifts the reassembled Body three times above the Paten [3].

The Spiritual Aspect in the Prayer of the Fraction (Al-Qisma)

  1. Separating the Spadikon (Al-Isbadikon) completely symbolizes that everyone left Him and fled. He bore all the pain alone. "I have trodden the winepress alone," (Isaiah 63:3).
  2. The dividing of the Oblation represents the sufferings of the Lord Jesus Christ in the flesh.
  3. The separations between the particles (Gawahir) represent the wounds of the Lord Jesus Christ. During this time, we must pray and lift up our hearts, asking with all our hearts that He accept us and forgive our sins. Remember the thief on the right cross who said, "Lord, remember me when You come into Your kingdom." (Luke 23:42-43).
  4. Furthermore, during the Fraction, we thank God that He gave me a second chance for repentance, that He bore my sins, and reconciled the heavenly with the earthly.
  5. Returning all the divided parts to one another, so the Oblation returns to its first shape, is tantamount to the union of the soul with the body at the Resurrection.
  6. Moving the particles placed on the left back to the right once again is tantamount to our transition from the "left" order to the "right" order.
  7. Dividing the Body in the shape of a cross is tantamount to the slaughtering.
  8. Separating the upper part above the Spadikon is tantamount to the piercing with the spear [4].
  9. Lifting the Body above the Paten (Siniya) three times after reassembling it:
    • Restoring the image of the Body to its first shape so that we may all be united—one spirit, one body, one mind. This is a symbol of the Church when she is one body and one love.
    • It is a confirmation of the prophecies: "Not one of His bones shall be broken." (John 19:36).
    • It also points to the Resurrection. He rose from the dead with a glorified body bearing only the scars of the nails and the spear [5].

And to our God be glory and honor forever. Amen.
