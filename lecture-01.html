1 - Raising of Incense for Matins & Vespers

The prayers of the Raising of Incense (Tasbeha) for Vespers (Ashia) and Matins (Baker) are among the prayers that the Church pays close attention to. Of course, the Divine Liturgy (Al-Quddas Al-Ilahi) is the most wonderful journey the Church takes us on to Heaven.

"When we stand in Your holy sanctuary, we are considered as standing in Heaven."

The Raising of Incense for Vespers and Matins is a prelude to the prayers of the Divine Liturgy. We notice that all prayers are chanted melodically, even a simple phrase like: "Peace be with all of you."[1]

Our Priest (Abouna)[2] says in the Liturgy: "You have given those on earth the praise of the Seraphim."

We also notice that the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal)[3] is at a higher level than the rest of the church because it represents the Holy of Holies[4]. And the Choir of Deacons (Khóros Al-Shamāmesa)[5] is higher than the nave of the church. This is because the Church wants to say that the Clergy (Al-Ikleerus)[6] are on a higher level spiritually.

Take note that the Raising of Incense is performed outside the Sanctuary. Why? Because the Altar of Incense (Madhbah Al-Bukhur)[7] was outside the Holy of Holies in the Tabernacle. Abouna prays with his head uncovered before the Lord, mirroring the elders "...casting their crowns before the throne."[8]

The Opening of the Curtain (Al-Sitr)

Abouna holds the Cross and opens the curtain from left to right. This signifies that God restored us from the "left" state to the "right" state[10]. He addresses the Father the Pantocrator (Dabit Al-Kol)[9].

Then, he begins the Prayer of Thanksgiving. He addresses the deacon, saying: "Pray." (Eshlil)[12]. At the end of the Prayer, he may mention the Liturgy of the Waters (Lakkan)[11] or a Wedding depending on the occasion.

Why the Right Side of the Altar?

After the Prayer of Thanksgiving, Abouna enters the Sanctuary with his right foot and stands to the right of the Altar. He places five hands[13] of incense. Every time Abouna makes these signs (Rushumat)[14], the deacon says "Amen."

Abouna prays inaudibly (Sirran)[15] during the Vespers Incense: "A poured-out ointment is Your Holy Name..."[16]

The Three Short Litanies

He prays the Three Short Litanies[17]: The Peace, the Fathers, and the Assemblies. During the Litany of Peace, he prays for the Catholic[18] and Apostolic Church. He moves from the North (Bahari)[22] to the South (Qibli)[19]. He prays for Pope Anba Tawadros II and Metropolitan Anba Serapion[20]. He asks for blessing for thousands of thousands and ten thousands[21] of ten thousands.

Procession and The Incense Cross

The Priest circles the church counter-clockwise[25]. Believers offer their prayers, which Abouna gathers in the Mystery of Return (Sirr Al-Raj'aa)[26]. He prays the Reverent Verses (Al-Arba' Al-Khushu'iyyat)[27], acknowledging Jesus as the One Hypostasis (Oqnum)[28].

Conclusion

The congregation begins the prayer "We Exalt You, O Mother of the True Light" followed by the Creed. Abouna begins the Supplication (Efnoti Nai Nan)[29]. The deacon carries the Cross and the Gospel (Bishara)[30]. Finally, he recites the prayer of Simeon the Elder[31]: "Lord, now You are letting Your servant depart in peace..."
