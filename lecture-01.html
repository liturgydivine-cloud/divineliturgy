Raising of Incense for Matins & Vespers

The prayers of the Raising of Incense (Tasbeha) for Vespers (Ashia) and Matins (Baker) are among the prayers that the Church pays close attention to. Of course, the Divine Liturgy (Al-Quddas Al-Ilahi) is the most wonderful journey the Church takes us on to Heaven. In the Divine Liturgy, we partake in glory and joy, and it is supposed to inform, enlighten, and make every person know about the life of Heaven. This is why in the prayer of the Third Hour, in the last section, we say:

"When we stand in Your holy sanctuary, we are considered as standing in Heaven."

And as we praise, we always remember Heaven. The Raising of Incense for Vespers and Matins is a prelude to the prayers of the Divine Liturgy. We notice that all prayers, whether in Vespers, Matins, or the Liturgy, are chanted melodically, even a simple phrase like: "Peace be with all of you." [1]

The prayers of Vespers and Matins are the preparation for receiving the King of Kings and Lord of Lords in the Divine Liturgy, when the bread is transformed into His pure Body and the wine into His precious Blood. The Divine Liturgy is a miniature picture of the life of Heaven, because in Heaven there is perpetual, unceasing praise. Our faith always holds that Heaven is constant praise to God. Our Priest (Abouna) [2] says in the Liturgy: "You have given those on earth the praise of the Seraphim."

We also notice that the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal) [3] is at a higher level than the rest of the church because it represents the Holy of Holies [4]. And the Choir of Deacons (Khóros Al-Shamāmesa) [5] is higher than the nave of the church. This is because the Church wants to say that the Clergy (Al-Ikleerus) [6] are on a higher level spiritually and must be at a high spiritual standard. This doesn't mean they are better than other people; no, they are servants of God first, and secondly, servants to you. It is also because the clergy appear before God in the prayers of the Church.

Take note that the Raising of Incense for Vespers and Matins is performed outside the Sanctuary. Why? Because the Altar of Incense (Madhbah Al-Bukhur) [7] was outside the Holy of Holies in the Tabernacle. That's why we notice Abouna praying and standing outside the Sanctuary, whether it's Vespers or Matins. And Abouna prays with his head uncovered before the Lord, as a sign that the priest, when praying before God, is exposed before Him. Certainly, each one of us has mistakes and weaknesses, but at this moment, he is exposed: "Before You, O Lord, and before You, nothing is hidden from You." Also, in the Book of Revelation, it speaks of the twenty-four elders: "...casting their crowns before the throne." [8] This is a form of reverence and solemnity. Therefore, at this moment, the person, whether the priest or the deacon, stands with reverence and solemnity because they are standing before the Holy of Holies, before the Divine Altar.

The Prayer of Matins (Baker) or Vespers (Ashia) Begins With the Opening of the Curtain (Al-Sitr)

Abouna holds the Cross in his hand and opens the curtain from left to right. Why? Because through the Cross, the door of Heaven was opened to us once again, and through the Cross, reconciliation occurred between the earthly and the heavenly. As Abouna opens the curtain, he says: "Have mercy upon us, O God the Father the Pantocrator (Dabit Al-Kol) [9], O Holy Trinity have mercy on us, O Lord, God of Hosts, be with us, for we have no other helper in our hardships and tribulations but You."

Furthermore, when Abouna opens the curtain from left to right, this signifies that God restored us from the "left" state to the "right" state [10]. Every movement that happens before us is not just a motion; no, it has a meaning and a beautiful arrangement derived from the Church Fathers, the received teaching (Al-Tasleem), and Tradition (Al-Taqleed). After that, Abouna offers a prostration (Metania) before the Sanctuary and before the congregation. Then, he begins the Prayer of Thanksgiving (Salat Al-Shukr), which is the introduction to all Church prayers, whether Matins, Vespers, the Liturgy, the Liturgy of the Waters (Lakkan) [11], Marriage, etc. Any Church service must begin with the Prayer of Thanksgiving.

Abouna begins by addressing the deacon, saying: "Pray." (Eshlil) [12]. The deacon then conveys this to the congregation by saying: "Stand up for prayer." (Epi prosevki stathite). At the end of the Prayer of Thanksgiving, in Matins and Vespers, Abouna says "...and for this church." In the Divine Liturgy, he says "...and for this holy priestly table." In any other prayer, for example in the Lakkan, he says "...for this Lakkan," or in a Crowning (Wedding), he says "...for these two newlyweds." In other words, he says the phrase that is appropriate for the specific occasion.

Why the Right Side of the Altar?

After the Prayer of Thanksgiving, Abouna enters the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal) with his right foot and stands to the right of the Altar (Al-Mazbah). The deacon presents the Censer (Shorya) to him, and Abouna places five hands [13] of incense into the censer while standing on the right side of the Altar.

The Five Hands of Incense:

  • The First Hand: Abouna says, "In the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, One God, Amen."
  • The Second Sign (Rashm) [14]: "Blessed is God the Father the Pantocrator, Amen." (Note: If there is a second priest partnering in the prayer, he places this second sign, called the "Hand of Blessing" or "Hand of Fellowship.")
  • The Third Sign (Rashm): "Blessed is His Only-Begotten Son, Jesus Christ our Lord, Amen."
  • The Fourth Sign (Rashm): "Blessed is the Holy Spirit the Comforter, Amen."
  • The Fifth Hand: "Glory and honor, honor and glory to the Holy Trinity, the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, now and at all times and unto the ages of all ages, Amen."

Every time Abouna makes these signs (Rushumat), the deacon holding the censer must say "Amen." Then, Abouna prays inaudibly (Sirran) [15] during the Vespers Incense, saying: "O Christ our God, the Great, the Awesome, the True, the Only-Begotten Son, Word of God the Father. A poured-out ointment is Your Holy Name, and in every place incense is offered to Your Holy Name and a pure offering." [16]

The deacon does not hear Abouna, but he is supposed to know where they are in the prayer because he has a response that he says inaudibly (Sirran) as well: "Pray for our sacrifice and for those who offered it." Abouna continues and says: "We ask You, O our Master, receive our supplications unto You. Let our prayer be set forth before You as incense, and the lifting up of our hands as the evening sacrifice. For You are the true Evening Sacrifice, who offered Yourself for our sins upon the honored Cross according to the will of Your Good Father, with whom You are blessed, with the Holy Spirit, the Giver of Life, who is of one essence with You, now and at all times and unto the ages of all ages, Amen."

And in the Raising of Matins Incense (Baker), Abouna prays the Litany (Oushia) of Matins Incense and says in it inaudibly (Sirran): "O God, who accepted unto Him the offerings of Abel the Just, the sacrifice of Noah and Abraham, and the incense of Aaron and Zacharias." The deacon is supposed to respond and say: "Pray for our sacrifice and for those who offered it." Abouna continues and says: "Accept unto You this incense from the hands of us sinners as a sweet fragrance of incense for the forgiveness of our sins with the rest of Your people. For blessed and full of glory is Your Holy Name, O Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, now and at all times and unto the ages of all ages, Amen."

The Three Short Litanies (Al-Awashi Al-Sighar)

After that, he prays the Three Short Litanies [17], which are: The Peace, the Fathers, and the Assemblies (Al-Ijtima'at). He does this while circling around the Altar (Al-Mazbah), and each time, the deacon responds with the response of the Litany. These are prayed inaudibly (Sirran) during the Verses of the Cymbals (Arba' Al-Naqous).

1. The Litany of Peace (Oushiat Al-Salama): Abouna prays while looking towards the East and says: "And we ask You, O our Master, remember, O Lord, the peace of Your One, Only, Holy, Catholic [18], and Apostolic Church." (The deacon responds inaudibly: "Pray for the peace of the One, Holy, Catholic, and Apostolic Orthodox Church of God.") Abouna moves to the South (Qibli) [19] side of the Altar while saying: "Which exists from one end of the world to the other." Once Abouna reaches the East of the Altar, he begins the Prayer of the Litany of the Fathers.

2. The Litany of the Fathers (Oushiat Al-Aba'): Abouna’s face is towards the West, and he says: "Remember, O Lord, our Patriarch, the honored Father, the High Priest, Pope Anba Tawadros II, and his partner in the apostolic ministry, our honored Father, Metropolitan Anba Serapion." [20] (The deacon responds: "Pray for our High Priest, Pope Anba Tawadros II... and his partner Metropolitan Anba Serapion, and all our Orthodox bishops.") Abouna continues and says: "Keep them safely for us for many years and peaceful times."

3. The Litany of the Assemblies (Oushiat Al-Ijtima'at): Abouna faces the East and says: "Remember, O Lord, our assemblies; bless them." (The deacon responds: "Pray for this holy church and our assemblies.") Abouna continues: "Grant that they may be unto us without obstacle or hindrance, that we may hold them according to Your holy and blessed will." Then he is at the East of the Altar, turns his face towards the West, incenses, and says: "Houses of prayer, houses of purity, houses of blessing, grant them to us, O Lord, and to Your servants who shall come after us forever." When he is at the West of the Altar, face towards the East, he says: "Arise, O Lord God, let all Your enemies be scattered, and let all who hate Your Holy Name flee before Your face." Then he moves to the East of the Altar, face towards the West, incenses, and says: "But let Your people be in blessing, thousands of thousands and ten thousands [21] of ten thousands doing Your will." Finally, reaching the West of the Altar, face towards the East, he incenses and says: "Through the grace, compassion, and love of mankind of Your Only-Begotten Son, our Lord, God, and Savior Jesus Christ."

The Incense Cross (Saleeb Al-Bukhur)

After that, Abouna offers incense in front of the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal). This is called the "Incense Cross" (Saleeb Al-Bukhur), and Abouna performs it three times. During the Incense Cross, the deacons chant the Verses of the Cymbals (Arba' Al-Naqous). He bows his head and offers three hands of incense (incensing three times), saying: "We worship You, O Christ, with Your Good Father and the Holy Spirit, for You have come (or risen, depending on the occasion/season) and saved us." "But as for me, I will come into Your house in the multitude of Your mercy; in fear of You, I will worship toward Your holy temple." "Before the angels, I will sing praise to You, and I will worship toward Your holy temple." (The third hand).

Then Abouna turns and looks to the North (Bahari) [22] side and incenses for the Virgin Mary, saying: "We give you peace with Gabriel the Angel, saying: Hail to you, O full of grace, the Lord is with you." Then Abouna turns and looks towards the West and says: "Peace to the choirs of angels, my lords the Apostles, the ranks of Martyrs, and all the Saints." Then Abouna turns to the South (Qibli) side, towards the icon of John the Baptist, gives the greeting of peace, and says: "Peace to John the Baptist; peace to John, son of Zacharias the Priest, son of the Priest." Finally, Abouna turns back towards the East and says: "Let us worship our Savior, the Good Lover of Mankind, for He came and saved us."

With this, the first Incense Cross is completed. After the Incense Cross, Abouna stands before the Sanctuary and prays:

  • In Vespers (Ashia): He prays the Litany of the Departed (Oushiat Al-Raqedeen). This is prayed throughout the year, whether the tune is Joyful (Farayhi) or Annual (Sanawi) [23]. Why does the Church remember the departed? To make the believers in the church feel the need for preparation, and that death is not death for Your servants, but a departure from the world to Paradise. I urge you to meditate on the words of the Litany of the Departed; you will find them very comforting, giving hope to every person that they have a place in Heaven.
  • In Matins (Baker): He prays the Litany of the Sick (Oushiat Al-Marda) and the Litany of the Travelers (Oushiat Al-Mosaferin) from Monday to Friday. The Church remembers the sick because the Church is considered a hospital for healing the soul, spirit, and body. That is why the Church also cares for the sick, prays for them, and gives them the healing medicine. And of course, for the travelers, she wishes and hopes for a journey full of safety and a safe return to their homes as well.

After the Litanies: Abouna enters the Sanctuary with his right foot, places a hand of incense, circles around the Altar, kisses the corner of the Altar before leaving, and exits the Sanctuary with his left foot. He begins to pray the Incense Cross a second time, with the same prayers and movements as explained the first time. This time, Abouna goes towards the Lectern (Manjalia) [24] and gives the greeting of peace to the Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ, saying: "Let us worship the Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ." He does this whether at the Coptic or Arabic lectern. After finishing the incense for the lectern, if there are relics (Ajsad) of saints, he gives a hand of incense, says the greeting of peace ("Hail..."), and mentions the name of the saint or martyr.

After that, he enters the Sanctuary to give a hand of incense to his brother priest serving with him, saying: "I ask you, my father, to remember me in your prayers." Abouna (the other priest) responds, saying: "The Lord preserve your priesthood like Melchizedek, Aaron, Zacharias, and Simeon, the priests of the Most High God." (Note: If it were in the Divine Liturgy, he says: "May God accept your sacrifice and remember the sacrifices offered in the Old Testament...").

The Procession of Incense among the People (Doret Bukhur Al-Sha'b)

After that, Abouna exits the Sanctuary and begins the Procession of Incense among the People. He says: "The blessing of the evening incense (or morning incense), may its holy blessing be with us, Amen." He begins to circle the church from left to right, counter-clockwise [25]. Why? Because the Church is above time, above the world, and does not let worldly matters enter inside her. She is a living Church, capable of swimming against the current. During this period, the deacons are chanting the Doxologies (Doxologiat) of the Virgin, the Angels, the Heavenly Beings, the Apostles, etc. In the procession, Abouna prays for the saints. (Take note: As Abouna passes by, everyone in the church is supposed to offer their prayers and requests. All these prayers are gathered, and Abouna takes them to offer them on the Altar in something called the Mystery of Return (Sirr Al-Raj'aa) [26]).

Usually, Abouna returns from the middle of the church, goes to the right, and circles back until he reaches the center of the church (in the last third of the nave). He begins to pray something called the Reverent Verses (Al-Arba' Al-Khushu'iyyat) [27], saying:

  • Facing East: "Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today, and forever, the One Hypostasis (Oqnum) [28], we worship Him and glorify Him. He offered Himself as an acceptable sacrifice on the Cross for the salvation of our race."
  • Turning towards the North (Bahari): "His Good Father smelled it at the time of evening on Golgotha."
  • Turning towards the West (facing the door): "And He opened for us the door of Paradise and restored our father Adam to his authority once again."
  • Incensing towards the South (Qibli): "Through His Cross and His Holy Resurrection, He restored man once again to Paradise."

Abouna then returns from the middle of the church—this is called the Mystery of Return (Sirr Al-Raj'aa). He enters the Sanctuary with his right foot and incenses over the Altar while praying: "O God who accepted unto Him the confession of the thief on the honored Cross, accept unto You the confession of Your people and forgive them all their sins for the sake of Your Holy Name which is called upon us. According to Your mercy, O Lord, and not according to our sins..." After that, he circles the Altar once, kisses it, goes down, stands before the Sanctuary door, and prays the Incense Cross (Saleeb Al-Bukhur). This is the third time. Then he enters the Sanctuary, gives a hand of incense to his brother priest, then to the deacons and the people, and stands beside the Altar until the end of the Doxologies.

The Conclusion of the Prayers

The deacons and the congregation begin the prayer "We Exalt You, O Mother of the True Light" (Nu'azzimuki), followed by the Creed (Qanun Al-Iman). After that, the deacon presents the Cross with three candles attached to it (symbolizing the Holy Trinity). Abouna signs the people three times and begins the Supplication Prayer for God's Mercy (Efnoti Nai Nan) [29]. Abouna: "O God, have mercy on us, settle Your mercy upon us, and have compassion on us..." People: "Amen." Abouna: "And hear us..." People: "Amen." Abouna: "Bless us, keep us, and help us." People: "Amen." Abouna: "Lift Your anger from us, visit us with Your salvation, and forgive us our sins." People: "Amen, Lord have mercy (Kyrie Eleison)" (three times).

Then the serving deacon presents the censer and the incense box (Haqq Al-Bukhur) to him. He places one hand of incense and begins the Litany of the Gospel (Oushiat Al-Ingil). The deacon is with him, carrying the Cross and the Gospel (Bishara) [30]. Then he enters the Sanctuary, places a hand of incense while saying: "Worship the Gospel of Jesus Christ." He circles around the Altar with the deacon carrying the Gospel (Bishara), while Abouna prays saying: "Lord, now You are letting Your servant depart in peace, According to Your word; For my eyes have seen Your salvation Which You have prepared before the face of all peoples, A light to bring revelation to the Gentiles, And the glory of Your people Israel." [31]

After that, Abouna incenses the Gospel three times while saying inaudibly (Sirran): "Worship the Gospel of Jesus Christ, the Son of the Living God, to Whom is glory forever, Amen." He takes the Gospel (Bishara) from the deacon and gives it to the serving priest or the other priests while saying: "But blessed are your eyes for they see, and your ears for they hear. May we be worthy to hear and to act according to Your Holy Gospels through the prayers of Your saints." The priests begin to kiss the Gospel while saying: "Worship the Gospel of Jesus Christ." After that, the deacon proclaims: "Stand in the fear of God and listen to the Holy Gospel." And the Gospel is read. At the end of the Gospel, Abouna gives three hands of incense (or simply incenses) and says inaudibly (Sirran): "You are He to whom glory is due, with one voice from everyone, and glory, honor, power, and worship, with Your Good Father and the Holy Spirit, the Giver of Life, who is of one essence with You, now and at all times and unto the ages of all ages, Amen." After that comes the Gospel Response, the Lord's Prayer, the Absolution, the Conclusion of the Prayers, the Blessing, the Lord's Prayer, and the Dismissal of the congregation.

(Note: In Matins (Baker), if the Liturgy follows immediately, Abouna says: "He who wants to leave..." implying those who are not staying for the Liturgy may go).

[4] Holy of Holies: This refers to the innermost part of the Temple. ↩

[8] Revelation 4:10: Biblical text regarding the act of reverence. ↩
[9] Pantocrator (Dabit Al-Kol): Standard theological term for Controller of All. ↩
[10] Left vs. Right: The "Left dispensation" refers to sin; the "Right" refers to being accepted. ↩
[11] Lakkan: Refers to the Liturgy of the Waters. ↩
[12] Eshlil / Epi prosevki stathite: Liturgical call to attention. ↩
[13] Hands vs. Spoonfuls: Liturgical term Ayadi Bukhur. ↩
[14] Signs (Rushumat): The sign of the cross made over incense to bless it. ↩
[15] Inaudibly (Sirran): Key term for prayers said silently by the clergy. ↩
[16] A poured-out ointment...: Reference to Song of Solomon 1:3 and Malachi 1:11. ↩
[17] Three Short Litanies: These are prayed silently by the priest. ↩
[18] Catholic: Meaning "Universal," not referring to the denomination. ↩
[19] North (Bahari) vs. South (Qibli): Directional liturgical terms used in Egypt. ↩
[20] Metropolitan Anba Serapion: Specific jurisdiction being prayed for. ↩
[21] Ten thousands (Ribwat): Archaic term meaning "myriads." ↩
[22] North (Bahari): See footnote 19. ↩
[23] Joyful/Annual Tune: Refers to the musical tone of the hymns. ↩
[24] Lectern (Manjalia): The stand where the holy readings are read. ↩
[25] Counter-clockwise: Classic contemplation on the Church being above time. ↩
[26] Mystery of Return (Sirr Al-Raj'aa): Silent prayer offering people's requests. ↩
[27] Reverent Verses (Al-Arba' Al-Khushu'iyya): Chanted while incensing the 4 directions. ↩
[28] Hypostasis (Oqnum): Theological term for "Person" of the Trinity. ↩
[29] Efnoti Nai Nan: Coptic hymn meaning "God have mercy on us." ↩
[30] Bishara: Metal case enclosing the Gospel book. ↩
[31] Simeon the Elder: Referring to the prayer recorded in Luke 2:29-32. ↩
