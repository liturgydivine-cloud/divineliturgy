Raising of Incense

Matins (Baker) & Vespers (Ashia)

The prayers of the Raising of Incense (Tasbeha) for Vespers (Ashia) and Matins (Baker) are among the prayers that the Church pays close attention to. Of course, the Divine Liturgy (Al-Quddas Al-Ilahi) is the most wonderful journey the Church takes us on to Heaven.

"When we stand in Your holy sanctuary, we are considered as standing in Heaven."

The Raising of Incense for Vespers and Matins is a prelude to the prayers of the Divine Liturgy. We notice that all prayers are chanted melodically, even a simple phrase like: "Peace be with all of you." [1]

The Sanctuary (Al-Haykal) [3] is at a higher level than the rest of the church because it represents the Holy of Holies [4]. And the Choir of Deacons (Khóros Al-Shamāmesa) [5] is higher than the nave of the church. This is because the Church wants to say that the Clergy (Al-Ikleerus) [6] are on a higher level spiritually.

Take note that the Raising of Incense is performed outside the Sanctuary. Why? Because the Altar of Incense (Madhbah Al-Bukhur) [7] was outside the Holy of Holies in the Tabernacle. Abouna [2] prays with his head uncovered, as a sign that the priest is exposed before Him. This mirrors the twenty-four elders: "...casting their crowns before the throne." [8]

The Opening of the Curtain (Al-Sitr)

Abouna holds the Cross and opens the curtain from left to right. This signifies that God restored us from the "left" state to the "right" state [10]. As he opens it, he addresses the Father Pantocrator [9].

He then begins the Prayer of Thanksgiving (Salat Al-Shukr). He addresses the deacon: "Pray" (Eshlil) [12], and the deacon responds: "Stand up for prayer." At the end of the prayer, specifically for the Liturgy of the Waters, he might say "...for this Lakkan" [11].

The Five Hands of Incense

Abouna stands to the right side of the Altar (Al-Mazbah), representing power and glory. He places five hands [13] of incense. Every time Abouna makes these signs (Rushumat) [14], the deacon says "Amen."

Abouna prays inaudibly (Sirran) [15]: "A poured-out ointment is Your Holy Name..." [16]

The Three Short Litanies

He prays the Three Short Litanies [17]: The Peace, the Fathers, and the Assemblies. During the Litany of Peace, he prays for the Catholic [18] Church. He moves between the South (Qibli) [19] and North (Bahari) [22] sides. He prays for Pope Anba Tawadros II and Metropolitan Anba Serapion [20]. He prays that the people be in blessing, thousands of thousands and ten thousands [21] of ten thousands.

The Incense Cross & Procession

Abouna performs the "Incense Cross" three times. In Vespers, he prays the Litany of the Departed. In Matins, he prays for the Sick and Travelers. He uses the Lectern (Manjalia) [24] for the Gospel readings.

During the procession, he circles the church counter-clockwise [25]. Believers offer their prayers, which Abouna gathers in the Mystery of Return (Sirr Al-Raj'aa) [26]. He then recites the Reverent Verses [27], acknowledging Jesus Christ as the same yesterday, today, and forever, the One Hypostasis (Oqnum) [28].

The Gospel & Conclusion

The service moves to the Supplication Prayer (Efnoti Nai Nan) [29]. The deacon carries the Cross and the Gospel (Bishara) [30]. Abouna recites the prayer of Simeon the Elder [31]: "Lord, now You are letting Your servant depart in peace..."

[9] Pantocrator (Dabit Al-Kol): Standard theological term for "Almighty." ↩
[10] Left vs. Right: "Left" refers to the state of sin; "Right" to the state of grace. ↩
[11] Lakkan: The Liturgy of the Waters (Epiphany, Apostles Feast, etc.). ↩
[12] Eshlil: Coptic/Greek liturgical calls to attention. ↩
[13] Hands of incense: (Ayadi Bukhur) even though a spoon is used. ↩
[14] Signs (Rushumat): The sign of the cross made over the incense. ↩
[15] Inaudibly (Sirran): Prayers said silently by the clergy. ↩
[16] Poured-out ointment: Reference to Song of Solomon 1:3. ↩
[17] Three Short Litanies: Prayed during the Verses of the Cymbals. ↩
[18] Catholic: Meaning "Universal," not the Roman Catholic denomination. ↩
[19] North (Bahari) vs. South (Qibli): Directional liturgical terms used in Egypt. ↩
[20] Metropolitan Anba Serapion: Specific jurisdiction noted (Diocese of Los Angeles). ↩
[21] Ten thousands (Ribwat): An archaic term for "myriads." ↩
[22] North (Bahari): See footnote 19. ↩
[24] Lectern (Manjalia): The wooden stand for holy readings. ↩
[25] Counter-clockwise: Signifying the Church is above time. ↩
[26] Mystery of Return: Offering the people's prayers to God. ↩
[27] Reverent Verses: Chanted while incensing the cardinal directions. ↩
[28] Hypostasis (Oqnum): Theological term for a "Person" of the Trinity. ↩
[29] Efnoti Nai Nan: "God have mercy on us." ↩
[30] Bishara: Metal case enclosing the Gospel book. ↩
