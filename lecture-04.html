Liturgy of the Word

Review

The Prayer of the Psalms (Agpeya) and the Choosing (Ikhtiyar) and Signs (Rushumat) of the Lamb:

Abouna begins the prayer after putting on the vestments of service. He starts with the Prayer of the Psalms, and the Lamb must be present, as I mentioned last time. Then I explained the Hours (Al-Sawa'i) that are read on regular days, on days with strict fasting (Sawm Inqita'i), during the Holy Fifty Days (Al-Khamasin), during Great Lent, and during Jonah's Fast. I will review them with you quickly:

  • Days with strict fasting (like Wednesday and Friday): We pray the 3rd, 6th, and 9th Hours.
  • Days without strict fasting (like Saturday, Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, and when there is no fasting): We read the Psalms of the 3rd and 6th Hours.
  • During Great Lent and Jonah's Fast: We pray the Hours from the 3rd Hour until Compline (Al-Nom).

We learned why we pray the Psalms: because they contain many prophecies about the Lord Jesus Christ, and each hour reminds us of the sufferings of the Lord Jesus Christ (and I gave examples). I also informed you that during the prayer "We Exalt You, O Mother of the True Light" (Nu'azzimuki) and the Creed (Qanun Al-Iman), Abouna washes his hands in preparation for choosing the Lamb, while reciting verses from Psalm 51 and Psalm 26 [1].

The Choosing (Ikhtiyar) and Signs (Rushumat) of the Lamb: When my father (Abouna) says:

"O Lamb of God, who takes away the sins of the world, hear us and have mercy on us,"

the deacons and the whole church begin to say "Lord have mercy" (Kyrie Eleison) 41 times, and we learned why.

Then we took an idea about the Oblation of the Lamb (Qurbanat Al-Hamal): What are the conditions for choosing the Lamb? The seals must be clear, there must be no defect from the baking, and the holes must be present and clear. Of course, we learned why the Lamb is round, the symbolism of wheat (Hinta), why no salt or sugar is added, and why we add yeast.

We also learned the conditions for the Abaraka (Wine): It must be from grape juice, naturally unfermented, and not cooked; just grape juice.

I will add something I might not have said regarding the deacons holding the water and the wine (Abaraka) and carrying a candle:

  • The candle signifies that the Lord Jesus Christ came as the Light of the World.
  • It also refers to the Angel Gabriel, the angel of the Annunciation, and the Angel Michael, the angel of the Resurrection.
  • Also, the Choosing of the Lamb symbolizes the birth of the Lord Jesus Christ.

After that, Abouna takes the Lamb and enters the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal). He begins to take water and baptize the Lamb, then wraps it in the Veil (Lafa'if), signifying the swaddling clothes or the burial shrouds. Then I told you that Abouna prays facing the people while carrying the Lamb. I explained this important prayer that Abouna says, in which he says "Glory and Honor," mentions the peace and edification of the Church, and remembers "those who brought to You these gifts, those on whose behalf they have been brought, and those by whom they have been brought." It is a beautiful prayer that gathers everything, including the people praying in the church.

After that, Abouna circles around the Altar. This signifies Simeon the Elder when he carried the Lord Jesus Christ in the Temple. The procession (Dora) signifies:

  1. The coming of the Lord Jesus Christ with His parents to the Temple to present Him to the Lord.
  2. The coming of the Lord Jesus Christ to the world once, offering Himself as a sacrifice for the world.
  3. The preaching and evangelism in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ to the whole world.

After the Signs of the Lamb, Abouna takes the flask of wine (Abaraka) from the deacon and begins the Prayer of Thanksgiving. Of course, I explained the Prayer of Thanksgiving to you.

After finishing it, Abouna covers the Paten (Siniya). This symbolizes:

  • The hiding of the Lord Jesus Christ and His flight to Egypt from Herod.
  • His going to Nazareth for fear of Archelaus, who reigned after Herod.
  • Symbolizing the burial of the Lord Jesus Christ in the tomb.

After that, they cover the Altar with the Prospharin (Al-Prospharin). This symbolizes the stone. The Veil (Lafa'if) with which the Lamb was wrapped is placed on the Prospharin, signifying the seals placed on the stone. This is called the Burial (Al-Dafna).

After the Burial, Abouna kisses the Altar and goes outside the Sanctuary along with the servants of the Altar, who symbolize Nicodemus, Joseph of Arimathea, Simon of Cyrene, and Gamaliel [2]. And he reads the Absolution of the Servants (Tahlil Al-Khudam).

We Continue Explaining the Liturgy: The Liturgy of the Catechumens (Quddas Al-Maw'uzin) or the Liturgy of the Word (Al-Quddas Al-Ta'limi)

Historical Note on the Liturgy of the Word: The Church took this idea from the story of the two disciples of Emmaus [3], when the Lord Jesus Christ walked with them and explained to them the Scriptures and the things concerning the Kingdom of Heaven, and what happened until the crucifixion of the Lord Jesus Christ and His resurrection. Then He gave them communion ("and He was known to them in the breaking of bread"), and He vanished from their sight.

From here, the Church took this beautiful event and incorporated it into the prayers of the Liturgy. Meaning, you learn from the readings that are read to you, and your eyes are opened to verses that affect your life. Then you enter into Communion, which is the gift that the Lord Jesus Christ prepares at the end of the Liturgy, so we may receive His Body and Blood for the forgiveness of our sins.

It was called the Liturgy of the Catechumens because, in the early Church, anyone who wanted to believe in Christ and become a Christian would attend the Liturgy and listen to the readings. After that, they would leave the church until they were baptized. Now, this system doesn't exist in the same way; anyone who wants to believe comes to the church, sits with the Father Priest first, and then with the servant who guides him. He attends the Liturgy, but he does not partake of Communion.

Let's Return to the Prayer of Absolution of the Servants (Tahlil Al-Khudam):

It is the absolution from the mouth of the priest, which is permission to participate in the service of the Divine Liturgy. No person should serve without taking absolution first. In the Prayer of Absolution, he mentions his brother priest serving with him, the deacons, the clergy, and all the people receive absolution, blessing, and forgiveness of their sins so they can complete the Liturgy with all purity.

Abouna asks for absolution from the Catholic Apostolic Church, our father Saint Mark the Apostle, our fathers the Apostles, our fathers the Saints whose names he mentions, our father the Patriarch, and his partner in the apostolic ministry, our father the Metropolitan or the Bishop.

Abouna mentions the people in the absolution so they can be positive and truly participate with all their hearts in the prayers of the Liturgy, not just listeners or present in body only while their thoughts are still attached to the world or somewhere else. (Forget the world and put it behind you as soon as you enter the church door).

After the Absolution, Abouna enters the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal) and stands to the right of the Altar.

The deacon presents the Censer (Al-Shorya) to him. Ideally, the highest-ranking deacon should present the censer to the Father Priest. He places five hands [spoonfuls] of incense into it, just as I taught you in the Prayer of Matins (Baker). But this time, it is called the Mystery of the Pauline Incense (Sirr Bukhur Al-Bolis).

He signs (Rashm) the incense box with the sign of the Cross and places five hands. Let me remind you of them:

  • First Hand: In the name of the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Spirit, One God, Amen.
  • Second Hand: Abouna places it if he is alone. But if there is a partner priest or more than one priest, he places the second hand. This is called the Hand of Blessing or the Hand of Fellowship. He says: "Amen. Blessed be God the Father the Pantocrator."
  • Third Hand: Abouna says: "Blessed be His Only-Begotten Son, Jesus Christ our Lord."
  • Fourth Hand: "Blessed be the Holy Spirit the Comforter."
  • Fifth Hand: He says: "Glory and honor, honor and glory to the Holy Trinity..."

Each time, the Serving Deacon says: "Amen."

Why does Abouna place five hands of incense? To remind us of those who offered incense and acceptable offerings to the Lord: Abel the Just, Noah the Righteous, Melchizedek the Priest, Aaron the Priest, and Zacharias the Priest.

The Mystery of the Pauline (Sirr Al-Bolis)

After that, Abouna prays the Mystery of the Pauline (Sirr Al-Bolis), saying inaudibly (Sirran):

"O God of knowledge, Giver of wisdom, who brings to light the hidden things of darkness, and gives the word to those who preach the gospel with great power; Who of Your goodness called Paul, who was for some time a persecutor, to be a chosen vessel; and was pleased in him, that he should become a chosen apostle and preacher of the Gospel of Your kingdom, O Christ our God.

We ask You also now, O Good One and Lover of Mankind, graciously grant us and all Your people a mind free from distraction and a pure understanding, that we may know and understand how profitable are Your holy teachings which are now read to us by him. And as he followed Your example, O Prince of Life, so make us also worthy to be like him in deed and in faith, glorifying Your Holy Name and glorying in Your Cross at all times. And unto You we send up glory and honor and worship, with Your Good Father and the Holy Spirit, the Giver of Life, who is of one essence with You, now and at all times and unto the ages of all ages. Amen."

Meaning: Abouna asks God for the blessing of the word found in the Pauline Epistle; that God may grant the souls of the listeners knowledge and wisdom, reveal to them the meaning of what they hear, and bless them with a mind undistracted by anything except the Word of God they are hearing—because the enemy of good wants to snatch it away. He asks God to give them a pure thought so they may act upon and understand the benefit of His holy teachings read to them, and to make them worthy to imitate Saint Paul the Apostle, who was a persecutor of Christianity for a long time, but God called him and chose him to be a chosen vessel, an apostle, and a preacher of the Gospel of the Kingdom.

After that, Abouna circles the Altar three times, as happened in the Matins Incense, saying the Litany of Peace, the Litany of the Fathers, and the Litany of the Assemblies.

Then, he exits the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal), performs the Incense Cross (Saleeb Al-Bukhur), and then circles the church from left to right. His descent here symbolizes Paul the Apostle in his preaching around the world [4]. We learned why he circles from left to right. The priest blesses the people with the Cross, saying:

"The blessing of Paul the Apostle be with you."

The beautiful thing at this moment: The deacons chant the hymn "This is the Censer of Pure Gold" (Tai Shori) [5], while Abouna holds the Censer (Shorya). It is a beautiful image from the Old Testament: the image of Aaron holding the golden censer, and the incense symbolizing the indwelling of the Lord Jesus Christ with us. Just as Moses the Prophet used to take the censer into the Tabernacle of Meeting, and the cloud covered the Tabernacle as a declaration of God's presence, the same situation applies to us: Abouna, the censer, and the abundant incense coming out of it symbolize God's presence with us. (There are many things about incense that need a separate lesson).

Abouna returns from the middle of the nave of the church, goes to the right, completes the procession, and returns from the center. This is called the Mystery of Return (Sirr Al-Raj'aa), in which he takes the prayers and requests of the people and places them before God on the Altar, aside from the papers given by the worshipers to Abouna.

The Hiteniat & Intercession

During this time, the deacons and the people pray the Hiteniat (Hymns of Intercession), in which they ask for the intercession of our Mother the Virgin, the Angels, and John the Baptist. Then they ask for the supplications and prayers of the Pure Apostles, our Father Saint Mark, the Martyrs, the Confessors, the prayers of the saints of this day, the prayers of our Father the Patriarch, and the prayers of our Father the Metropolitan or Bishop.

These prayers connect the Victorious Church in Heaven and the Struggling Church on earth. We ask for their intercessions, supplications, and prayers on our behalf because they appear before God, and their intercession and supplication are acceptable. We take as an example of this the intercession of the Virgin Mary at the Wedding in Cana of Galilee. (The subject of Intercession is a separate lesson we might take in the future).

Abouna in the Mystery of Return (Sirr Al-Raj'aa):

When Abouna returns and stands before the Altar, he says:

"O Lord, who accepted the confession of the thief on the wood of the Cross, accept unto You the confessions of Your people and forgive us our sins."

Abouna asks for the forgiveness of sins, places them before God on the Altar, and prays the requests he received from the people. Then Abouna circles around the Altar, descends again from the Sanctuary, performs the Incense Cross (previously explained in the Raising of Incense for Vespers and Matins), enters to give the hand of blessing to his colleague sharing in the service, and gives the censer to the deacon. With this, the Pauline Procession is finished.

A Simplified Idea About the Epistles of St. Paul the Apostle

This is the last thing I will say in today's lesson, but you must take a simplified idea about the Epistles of St. Paul the Apostle. So when you hear the name of the Epistle, you know where he wrote it and why, or the reason:

  • Epistle to the Romans: He spoke about the righteousness of God, God's promises, God's judgment, God's wrath on the ungodliness of men, and the laws of Christian life. He wrote it in Corinth, and it contains 16 chapters.
  • First Epistle to the Corinthians: He speaks about unity in the Church and how to solve problems in the Church with love. He wrote it in Ephesus, and it has 16 chapters.
  • Second Epistle to the Corinthians: He speaks about the ministry of reconciliation, solving problems between brothers, defending himself and his apostleship, and requesting help for the Church of Jerusalem. He wrote it in Macedonia, and it has 13 chapters.
  • Epistle to the Galatians: In it, he reveals the true Gospel, the Cross, true freedom, rebukes the Church of Galatia, and speaks of the fruits of the Holy Spirit. It is in 6 chapters.
  • Epistle to the Ephesians: He speaks about the Lord Jesus Christ in the Church, the Church as His Body, the Church as a building, believers, and priesthood. He wrote it in Rome, and it has 6 chapters.
  • Epistle to the Philippians: He shows that our life in Christ equals joy because He is our life, our example, our goal, our strength, and our salvation. He wrote it in Rome, and it has 4 chapters.
  • Epistle to the Colossians: He speaks of Christ as the Head of the Church and us as the Body. He wrote it in Rome, and it has 4 chapters.
  • First Epistle to the Thessalonians: He speaks about the holy life that leads to eternal life. He wrote it in Corinth, and it has 5 chapters.
  • Second Epistle to the Thessalonians: He speaks about the coming of the Lord Jesus Christ so that we are not shaken, giving signs of the Second Coming, the most important of which is the Great Apostasy. He wrote it in Corinth, and it is 3 chapters.
  • First Epistle to Timothy: In it, he spoke about pastoral care and advice to him on how to shepherd the people. He wrote it in Macedonia, and it has 6 chapters.
  • Second Epistle to Timothy: He speaks about overcoming difficulties in ministry and a farewell to his disciple because he sensed the time of his departure. It consists of 4 chapters [6] and he wrote it in Rome [7].
  • Epistle to Titus: He speaks to his disciple Titus about the bishop's management of the Church for the salvation of the people. It consists of 3 chapters.
  • Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Philemon: He speaks about brotherhood among us in Christ Jesus, Philemon's love and work in the Church, and requests forgiveness and pardon for his son Onesimus, whom he begot in his chains, showing the extent of the humility of our teacher Paul the Apostle. It is 1 chapter.
  • Epistle of our Teacher Paul the Apostle to the Hebrews: This is an important epistle he wrote to the apostatizing Christian Jews and his Jewish brethren to show them that our Lord Jesus Christ is All in All. He is greater than Moses, Joshua, and Aaron; He is the Greatest Temple and the Greatest Sacrifice. When He offered Himself as a sacrifice on the Cross, all sacrifices ceased. Because the Temple still existed and sacrifices were still being offered, but they were rejected. Until the writing of this epistle, this made some believing Jews doubt, but he showed them that the day of this Temple is coming to be destroyed as Jesus Christ said. It is one of the powerful epistles of our teacher Paul the Apostle because he connected the Old Testament and the New Testament in a style full of argument and persuasion. The Epistle has 13 chapters.

Glory be to God always and forever. Amen. Pray for me.
