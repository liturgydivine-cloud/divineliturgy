The Lord's Prayer

Summary of the Previous Lesson:

Last time, I spoke with you about the ritual stages (Marahil Taqsiyya) of dividing the Holy Body. They are two stages: the first stage is the Prayer of Institution (Salat Al-Ta'sees), and the second stage takes place after the descent of the Holy Spirit. The Fraction (Al-Qisma) after the descent of the Holy Spirit varies according to the occasion in the Church.

During the Fraction, after each section, the people pray: "Kyrie Eleison, Kyrie Eleison, Lord have mercy." As I said, the tune is mournful (Hazayni) throughout the year because these are the moments of the sufferings and wounds of the Lord Jesus Christ.

There are two rites for the Fraction in the Coptic Church: the Connected Fraction (Al-Qisma Al-Muttasila) and the Separated Fraction (Al-Qisma Al-Munfasila). I explained both in detail with illustrative diagrams; you can return to that lesson for review.

The Spiritual Aspect of the Prayer of the Fraction:

  1. Separating the Spadikon (Al-Isbadikon) completely symbolizes that everyone left Him and fled.
  2. Dividing the Oblation (Qurbana) represents the sufferings of the Lord Jesus Christ in the flesh.
  3. The separations represent the wounds and pains of these wounds for the Lord Jesus Christ.
  4. The moments of the Fraction require every one of us to return to ourselves and offer sincere repentance to the One who bought us with His Blood, remembering the thief on the right who won Paradise with a single word.
  5. During the Fraction, we thank God because He gave me a second chance for repentance, bore my sins, opened the door of Paradise once again through the Cross, and reconciled the heavenly with the earthly.
  6. Dividing the Body in the shape of a cross is tantamount to the slaughtering.
  7. Separating the upper part above the Spadikon is the symbol of the piercing with the spear... etc. (Return to the previous lesson to complete the spiritual aspect of the Holy Fraction).

The spiritual meaning of lifting the Body above the Paten (Siniya) three times after reassembling it:

  • Reassembling the divided parts back together so the Oblation returns to its original shape symbolizes that we are all united without division—one spirit, one body, one mind—because we partook of one Body.
  • It is a confirmation of the prophecies that "not one of His bones shall be broken." (John 19:36).
  • It also points to the Resurrection of the Lord of Glory from the dead with a glorified body in which there are no fresh wounds, except the scars of the nails and the spear.

The Lord's Prayer ("Our Father") & The Secret Prayers

By the grace of God, today we will take the Lord's Prayer ("Our Father") after the Fraction, and the secret prayers that Reverend Abouna prays.

After the "Our Father," there are long responses from the deacons outside the Sanctuary (Al-Haykal) and from the Serving Deacon inside the Sanctuary. The reason is to give Abouna sufficient time to finish the secret prayers he prays during these responses.

The Importance of the Lord's Prayer after the Fraction: It is known about the Lord's Prayer (Al-Salat Al-Rabbaniyya) that it is a complete prayer that reveals our weaknesses as humans. It contains a warning against not forgiving the trespasses of others when we say, "And forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us." This is a confession that we are reconciled with ourselves and with others, and there is no hatred or grudge against anyone. Because you are going in to partake of Communion (Al-Tanawul) and take the Body and Blood of Christ. To be worthy of this Communion, remember the saying of Christ to us: "If you bring your gift to the altar, and there remember that your brother has something against you... first be reconciled to your brother." (Matthew 5:23-24).

The Lord Jesus Christ said, "When you pray, say: Our Father in heaven..." [1] Of course, in the Liturgy, through the sacrifice present on the Holy Altar, He gave us the specific sonship of the Lord Jesus Christ, and we became His brothers. That is why we are able to say, "Our Father who art in heaven," and He is the one who taught us this prayer, and to Him be the glory, He said, "When you pray, say: Our Father."

The Lord's Prayer demonstrates our obedience to the teachings and commandments of the Holy Bible. Because it contains everything we need from our Lord who is present before us, dwelling before us on the Holy Altar. That is why we present the requests we ask for, because we believe and see on the Holy Altar that He will give us Himself; therefore, He hears all the requests when the whole church prays, "Our Father who art in heaven." (There are many things and deep meanings in the "Our Father" prayer that need a separate lesson on their own).

The meaning when we say, "Give us this day our bread of tomorrow" [2]: The Eucharist (Eucharistia) is the Bread of the life to come, but we partake of it in our present life, live by it, and are nourished by it day by day until we reach our eternal inheritance and the eternal life prepared for us.

And when we say, "Give us this day our daily bread": It means this Bread (Communion) is our sustenance, sufficient for us throughout our lives, helping us against the snares of the enemy until we reach eternal life.

A very important point is that the Eucharist is not of this age, but of the age to come. However, it is the earnest (down payment / Arboun) of eternal life. Because Christ abides in us and we abide in Him upon Communion, and this is the earnest of eternal life and heavenly good things. It is considered provisions (Zad) that nourish us on the path of our struggle until we reach the bosom of the Father. That is why the Lord's Prayer is very important after the Fraction. And as I said, we must implement the teachings of God found in this prayer.

The Deep Inaudible Prayers (Salawat Sirriyya)

At the end of the Lord's Prayer, the deacons outside the Sanctuary say "In Christ Jesus our Lord" in a long, melismatic tune (Lahn), and the Serving Deacon inside the Sanctuary responds in a long tune: "Bow your heads to the Lord." [3]

From this moment, Reverend Abouna begins the beautiful, deep, and meaningful inaudible prayers (Salawat Sirriyya), and I urge you to contemplate their words. The long hymns help Abouna finish these secret prayers for us during this period. Because we are about to enter the most important part of the Divine Liturgy (Al-Quddas Al-Ilahi), which is the meeting with the Bridegroom, and partaking of the Holy Body and Precious Blood. Because after the secret prayers and the signs (Rushumat), the distribution of the Body and Blood begins, which is the time of Communion (Al-Tanawul).

1. The Prayer of the Father (Salat Al-Aab)

"Yea, we ask You, O Holy, Good Father, who loves goodness, lead us not into temptation, nor let any iniquity rule over us. But deliver us from worthless works, and their thoughts, their movements, their sights, and their touch. The tempter, abolish him and drive him away from us. Rebuke also his movements planted within us, and cut off from us the causes that lead us to sin. And deliver us by Your holy power, in Christ Jesus our Lord, through whom is the glory and honor forever. Amen." [4]

Reverend Abouna here—whom God chose and prepared for this service for our sake—prays and asks, "Lead us not into temptation, nor let any iniquity rule over us," just as Your people prayed in "Our Father." "Deliver us, O Lord, from worthless works." Sin begins with a thought, then execution (movement), and its sights and touches remain stuck in our thoughts and our eyes. So, O Lord, abolish and distance the enemy of good from us. Drive him away. Rebuke him and remove the things he has planted in us (the nature of earthly man). Cut off everything that leads us to sin, for You are mighty in battle.

After this prayer, the deacon inside the Sanctuary says, "Bow your heads," in the long tune (Lahn), so that Abouna can begin the second prayer, the Prayer of Submission to the Father.

2. The Prayer of Submission to the Father (Salat Al-Khudou' lil-Aab)

"The graces of the beneficence of Your Only-Begotten Son, our Lord, God, and Savior Jesus Christ, have been fulfilled. We have confessed His saving passion, we have preached His death, we have believed in His resurrection, and the Mystery is accomplished.

We give thanks to You, O Lord God the Pantocrator, because Your mercy is great toward us, for You have prepared for us those things which the angels desire to look into [5]. We ask and entreat Your goodness, O Lover of Mankind, that as You have purified us all, You would unite us to Yourself through our partaking of Your divine mysteries. That we may be filled with Your Holy Spirit and established in Your orthodox faith. And having been filled with the longing for Your true love, we may declare Your glory at all times, through Christ Jesus our Lord..."

Before the Prayer of Submission to the Father, the Serving Deacon inside the Sanctuary says, "Bow your heads to the Lord," in the long tune, so that Abouna can finish this prayer. At the same time, this secret prayer is called the Prayer of Submission to the Father, which is why we must all bow our heads in a state of humility before God.

Because Abouna says: "The graces of the beneficence of Your Only-Begotten Son, our Lord, God, and Savior Jesus Christ, have been fulfilled." What does this mean? It means the Mystery has been fulfilled and completed by the Holy Spirit, transforming the Oblation (Qurban) and the wine into the true Body and Blood of the Lord Jesus Christ, our Savior.

3. The Absolution to the Father (Tahlil lil-Aab)

You will find the Serving Deacon saying, "Let us attend in the fear of God," in a long tune, also to give Abouna a chance to pray this Absolution. This prayer is very beautiful and teaches us how to pray. We find that the previous prayers were a preparation for this deep prayer in which he says, "O God, absolve us and absolve Your people," and mentions his own weakness before God.

Let's see what the prayer says [6]:

"O Master, Lord God the Pantocrator, Healer of our souls, bodies, and spirits. You are He who said to our father Peter, through the mouth of Your Only-Begotten Son, our Lord, God, and Savior Jesus Christ: 'You are Peter, and on this rock I will build My church, and the gates of Hades shall not prevail against it. And I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven, and whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven.' (Matthew 16:18-19). Let, O my Master, Your servants—my fathers, my brethren, and my weakness—be absolved by my mouth through Your Holy Spirit, O Good One and Lover of Mankind.

O God, who takes away the sin of the world, hasten to accept the repentance of Your servants, granting them the light of knowledge and the forgiveness of sins. For You are a compassionate and merciful God; You are longsuffering, abundant in mercy, and righteous. And if we have sinned against You in word or in deed, pardon and forgive us, as a Good One and Lover of Mankind. O God, absolve us and absolve all Your people...

Remember, O Lord, my own weakness also, and forgive my many sins; and where sin abounded, let Your grace abound much more. Because of my own sins and the impurities of my heart, do not withhold from Your people the grace of Your Holy Spirit. O God, absolve us and absolve all Your people from every sin, from every curse, from every denial, from every false oath, and from every encounter with heretics and pagans. Grant us, O our Master, a mind, power, and understanding to flee completely from every evil deed of the adversary, and grant us to do what is pleasing to You at all times.

Write our names with the choirs of Your saints in the kingdom of heaven, through Christ Jesus our Lord..."

What do you think of this prayer coming from the mouth of Reverend Abouna by the Holy Spirit? Because he is the one completing the Holy Mystery, as God chose him for this: "And no man takes this honor to himself, but he who is called by God." (Hebrews 5:4).

The Conclusion of the Absolutions & The Diaconal Responses

With this, Abouna finishes the secret prayers (Salawat Sirriyya), and then he begins the Three Short Litanies: the Litany of Peace, the Litany of the Fathers, and the Litany of the Assemblies. He says them audibly (Gahran). He only says the introduction of the Litanies:

  • In the Litany of Peace, he says: "Remember, O Lord, Your One, Only, Holy, Catholic, and Apostolic Church..."
  • In the Litany of the Fathers, he says: "Remember, O Lord, our High Priest, the Honored Pope Anba Tawadros, and his partner in the apostolic ministry, our Father Metropolitan Anba Serapion."
  • (And as I said, he says the Litany of the Assemblies audibly).

The deacon responds to him and says: "You have truly saved [us]. And with your spirit. Let us attend in the fear of God." [7]

There are three things the deacon says here:

  1. "You have truly saved [us]" (Khallasta Haqqan): (Not "Khullista" - You were saved). Here he is addressing our Lord, that He accomplished salvation on the Cross, abolished death for us, opened Paradise through the Cross, and gave us eternal life. When we partake of Communion (Al-Tanawul), He also gives us the earnest (Arboun) of eternal life. Meaning, He completed the salvation for our father Adam and restored him once again to Paradise. After redemption was accomplished on the Cross, it became the right of Adam and Eve to eat from the Tree of Life, which is Communion. Meaning, salvation has opened Paradise before us.
  2. "And with your spirit": This is directed to the Father Priest. Meaning, he has a portion just like Adam; he has a portion in salvation and eternal life.
  3. "Let us attend in the fear of God": He is addressing the people and directing it to the people. Meaning, pay attention: the moment coming up next is very important. You must stand in fear, reverence, and attention.

That is why the people respond and say, "Lord have mercy, Lord have mercy, Lord have mercy," three times.

And to our God be all glory and honor forever. Amen.

Footnotes

[1] Our Father: Privilege of adoption granted only to the faithful. Traditionally only taught after baptism in the early Church. ↩
[2] Bread of Tomorrow: From the Greek epiousion, referring to the super-substantial Bread of the Kingdom given to us today in the Eucharist. ↩
[3] Bowing the Head: Ancient liturgical command preparing the congregation to receive the final blessing and absolution. ↩
[4] Prayer of the Father: A deep expansion of "Deliver us from the evil one," detailing the progression of sin from thought to touch. ↩
[5] "Things which the angels desire to look into": Taken from 1 Peter 1:12, emphasizing the magnitude of the Eucharist. ↩
[6] The Absolution to the Father: The final powerful absolution where the priest invokes the authority of "binding and loosing" right before Communion. ↩
[7] "Saved" vs. "Were Saved": Addressing Christ present on the altar, acknowledging the Eucharist as the restored Tree of Life. ↩
